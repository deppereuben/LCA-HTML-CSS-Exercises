My Tech Blog

A review of two of my favourite technologies:

1.VolkanoX VXH100 Bluetooth Headphones

The VolkanoX VXH100 Bluetooth Headphones are a solid budget-friendly option for everyday use. They offer active noise cancelling (ANC), which helps reduce background noise—useful for commuting, studying, or working in busy environments. While the ANC isn't premium-level, it performs well for the price range.

Sound quality is generally clear with decent bass, making them suitable for music, podcasts, and calls. They feature Bluetooth connectivity (up to ~10m range) and include a built-in microphone for hands-free use.

One of the standout features is the battery life, offering up to 20 hours with ANC on and up to 50 hours without it, which is impressive for affordable headphones. The foldable design also makes them easy to carry around.

Overall, a great value-for-money choice if you want wireless headphones with ANC, long battery life, and everyday comfort without spending too much.

2.HP EliteBook 840 G5 Intel i5, 8th Gen Ultrabook Laptop

The HP EliteBook 840 G5 is a reliable business-class ultrabook that still holds up well for everyday tasks. Powered by an 8th Gen Intel Core i5 processor with 4 cores, it delivers smooth performance for productivity work like browsing, Microsoft Office, and light multitasking.

One of its biggest strengths is the premium build quality. The slim, lightweight design (around 1.4-1.5 kg) makes it easy to carry, while the sturdy chassis gives it a professional feel. The 14-inch Full HD display is clear and anti-glare, which is great for working long hours.

In terms of usability, the laptop offers a comfortable keyboard, good battery life, and strong security features, making it ideal for students and office use. It's also upgrade-friendly, with additional RAM slots and SSD options available.

However, it's not perfect. The integrated graphics mean it's not suitable for heavy gaming or intensive design work, and the display brightness and color accuracy are fairly average. Some older or refurbished units may also show signs of wear or reduced battery health.

Overall, a solid, affordable ultrabook that's best suited for work, studying, and everyday computing, especially if you value durability and reliability over high-end performance.
